# CIRO Supervisor Exam — Transition Rules from the Old Regime & Legacy Study Materials

Research date: June 4, 2026. All claims sourced; unverifiable items flagged at the end.

### 1.1 Grandfathering — "Continuing in the same role" (IDPC s. 2625(2))
- Individuals approved before January 1, 2026 are NOT subject to the new proficiency requirements (including the Supervisor Exam) if they continue in the same role. They must still complete the new mandatory Conduct Training under s. 2604(2) (existing Approved Persons: deadline December 31, 2026; failure = automatic suspension).
- "Same role" operationally: an individual is deemed to have continued in the same role as long as their approval did not cease for longer than 180 days. Return within 180 days to the same category → exempt from new proficiencies. Return after more than 180 days → subject to the new requirements (incl. Supervisor Exam).
- Same category matters: FAQ #2 — a CCO who ceased approval Dec 1, 2025 and seeks reapproval as a Supervisor on Feb 2, 2026 does NOT qualify (different role) and "would be subject to complete the new proficiencies to be a Supervisor," even within 180 days.
- FAQ #7 — an existing Supervisor (over RRs/IRs, institutional securities) as of Jan 1, 2026 is exempt from "the Supervisors Exam, CIRE and the Institutional Securities Exam" as long as approval doesn't lapse more than 180 days. Confirms the new pre-approval bundle for that supervisor type. The supervisor's exemption also covers downgrading to RR (institutional) without new exams (though the new education/experience requirement for RR still applies).
- Leaves of absence (FAQ #3): if the person remains an Approved Person during the LOA, they are continuing in the role (CE/training obligations continue). If approval ceases >180 days, they must requalify under Rule 2600; discretionary exemption may be sought.

### 1.2 Re-approval after absence — CIRE exemption (clause 2625(2)(i))
- If someone misses the 180-day window: exempt from the CIRE if they have minimum two years' prior experience in the same Approved Person category within the three years before application. All other pre-/post-approval requirements still apply (a returning Supervisor candidate would still need the Supervisor Exam and any underlying product exam, but not the CIRE).
- FAQ #9 example: RR approved May 2021–May 2026, retires, returns April 2027 → CIRE-exempt (2 yrs in last 3), but must still write the Retail Securities Exam.

### 1.3 Transition window for old CSI courses (s. 2629(1)) — the "IDSC pipeline" rule
A person can qualify under the OLD (pre-2026) proficiency requirements instead of the new exams only if ALL four conditions are met:
1. Enrolled in the prescribed CSI course(s) (e.g., IDSC for supervisors) **before January 1, 2026**;
2. Successfully completes the course(s) and exam(s) **before January 1, 2027**;
3. Upon completion would have satisfied the pre-Jan-1-2026 proficiency requirements for the same category sought; and
4. The sponsoring dealer **files the approval application on NRD before January 1, 2027**.

Collateral facts:
- The CSI contract expired December 31, 2025; "individuals who enroll in a CSI course prior to January 1, 2026, will have up until December 31, 2026, to complete any exams offered through the CSI" (GN-2600-25-001 §3.5).
- CSI's own notice: course extensions on mandatory courses granted only up to December 31, 2026 — no extensions beyond. https://www.csi.ca/en/ciro
- FAQ #19: meeting 2629(1) exempts the candidate from the whole of s. 2603 — both the new exams AND the new minimum education/experience requirements.
- Nuance (Existing Approved Persons page): 2629(1) is course-recognition transition, NOT an exemption from category changes — e.g., options-only/futures-only approval categories ceased to exist Jan 1, 2026 regardless. https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/existing-approved-persons

### 1.4 Validity periods — old courses vs. new exams
- Old course validity is DEAD: FAQ #14 — "course validity no longer applies once the new rules are implemented." Pre-2026, CSI courses (incl. IDSC) were valid 3 years from completion or last relevant approval (old s. 2628; IIROC GN-2600-20-001 §8.3). After Jan 1, 2026, an expired/never-used IDSC has no rule-based recognition — only s. 2625 grandfathering or discretionary exemption (GN-2600-25-001 §3.4.2).
- New exam validity (s. 2628): a CIRO exam is valid if (a) passed within 3 years before applying, (b) previously approved in the same category (or one requiring the same exam) within 3 years, or (c) one year of relevant securities-industry experience within the 3 years before applying. FAQ #13: pass the Supervisor Exam May 1, 2026 → must be approved as Supervisor by May 1, 2029, else rewrite (unless the 1-year-experience saver applies). Suspension/non-activity periods don't count.
- No validity period for the CFA Charter and CIM designation while in good standing (s. 2628(3)).

### 1.5 Other recognition/exemption provisions relevant to supervisors
- FINRA/NFA (s. 2627): FINRA registrants in similar capacity within 3 years + applicable FINRA exams → CIRE-exempt. Derivatives: FINRA+NFA options/futures registration within 3 years + Series 3 and Series 7 → exempt from the Derivatives Exam when applying as RR/IR-derivatives or as a Supervisor over them (guideline: ≥12 months options+futures experience).
- Derivatives consolidation (s. 2625(3)): options-only and futures-only Approved Persons map to "(legacy)" product types; anyone newly seeking to supervise or deal in any derivatives must complete the single Derivatives Exam.
- WME transitional flexibility (s. 2629(2)): RRs with a WME post-approval obligation may complete WME by the earlier of their due date or Dec 31, 2026, or substitute the Retail Securities Exam (FAQs #21–23).
- Discretionary exemptions remain available case-by-case (GN-2600-25-001 §4.2).

## 2. OLD REGIME — pre-2026 supervisor proficiency path (verified course names)

Primary sources: IIROC GN-2600-20-001 "Plain Language Rule Book Project – Registration Changes" (June 1, 2020) — https://www.ciro.ca/media/2797/download?inline=1 — and IIROC's official list of regulatory courses (Appendix B to Notice 15-0215) — https://www.ciro.ca/media/2210/download?inline=1 — plus CSI course pages.

### 2.1 Verified course-name history
- **IDSC = Investment Dealer Supervisors Course, formerly the Branch Managers Course (BMC)**. Verified twice: CSI's IDSC page meta ("Formerly BMC", https://www.csi.ca/en/learning/courses/idsc) and IIROC GN-2600-20-001 footnote 5.
- The **Effective Management Seminar (EMS)** was a SEPARATE course, not a prior name of the IDSC — a post-licensing requirement for Supervisors (due within 18 months of approval) under the old Dealer Member Rules; removed as a regulatory requirement June 1, 2020 (GN-2600-20-001 §4.4).
- **PDO = Partners, Directors and Senior Officers Course** — also separate. Historically acceptable supervisor proficiency under old Rule 2900, but as of June 1, 2020 "The PDO is no longer an acceptable proficiency [for Supervisors], as it does not sufficiently address the Supervisor competencies" (GN-2600-20-001 §4.2). PDO remained the course for Executives/Directors/CFOs until end-2025. Lineage: **BMC → IDSC**, with EMS and PDO as distinct courses that fell away in 2020.

### 2.2 Pre-2026 supervisor requirement set (IIROC Rule 2602(3), carried into IDPC Rules Jan 1, 2023 – Dec 31, 2025)
- Baseline supervisors (over RRs/IRs, retail and — from June 2020 — institutional): CSC + CPH + **IDSC** + two years of relevant experience (investment dealer, or per clause 2602(3)(xvii): mutual fund dealer, NI 31-103 portfolio manager, or recognized foreign SRO entity). Designated Supervisors for new accounts/account activity, discretionary accounts, and advertising/sales-literature pre-approval: same IDSC + 2-years table (GN-2600-20-001 §5.1).
- Options supervisors: options proficiency (DFC + OLC, or combined DFOL) plus the **Options Supervisors Course (OPSC)**, on top of CSC/CPH. The OPSC curriculum includes a "Supervisors Proficiency Requirements" chapter (GN-2600-20-001 §7.2–7.3; https://www.csi.ca/en/learning/courses/opsc/curriculum).
- Futures supervisors: futures proficiency (DFC + FLC, or DFOL in lieu of DFC) plus the **Canadian Commodity Supervisors Exam (CCSE)** (IIROC course list; CSI catalogue https://www.csi.ca/en/learning/courses/ccse/).
- Supervisors of managed accounts: PM-level baseline (CIM or CFA Charter + RIME) (§5.2). Supervisors of research reports: CFA Level III/Charter or equivalent + 2 years (§5.3).
- NEC (New Entrants Course) accepted for foreign-SRO transferees, including retail/institutional Supervisor approval (§7.6).
- IIROC's June 2020 rules had their own "continuing in the same role" exemption with a 90-day reinstatement window — the 2026 model's 180-day window is more generous (§8.1).

### 2.3 Content mapping: legacy courses → new Supervisor Exam
The new Supervisor Exam is a single exam for ALL supervisor types ("one exam applicable to all types of Supervisors on the basis that we have one competency profile for all Supervisors"), with underlying product exams added by activity supervised. Source: Bulletin 24-0206 (https://www.bcsc.bc.ca/-/media/PWS/New-Resources/Industry/Marketplaces-SROs-and-Market-Infrastructure/SROs/CIRO-Proposed-Rule-AmendmentsApproved-Persons-under-IDPC-Rules-Request-for-comment-July-4-2024.pdf).

- **IDSC → strongest single match.** Its 12 chapters (role of supervisor; supervision structures; regulatory framework; gatekeeper; risk management; account opening/updating; conflicts/disclosures; maintaining client accounts incl. KYC/suitability/trade reviews; investment banking & trade desk supervision; client complaints; dealing with regulators) line up almost one-to-one with exam elements 1–9.
- **CPH** → underpins Element 1 and conduct/complaint-handling material, but most CPH content now lives in the CIRE/Conduct Training.
- **OPSC** chapters 4–7 (supervisor responsibilities, account opening/approval under Rule 3252, daily/monthly options reviews, complaints) → overlap Elements 4–5; strategy chapters (1–3) map to the new Derivatives Exam.
- **CCSE** → futures-account supervision content overlaps Elements 4–5; product content maps to the Derivatives Exam.
- **PDO** → maps mainly to the new Executive/Director side, not the Supervisor Exam.
- Contrast — the legacy IDSC exam was **2 hours / 75 MCQs / 60% pass** with five topic blocks (Supervision Requirements & Skills 19%; Rules, Responsibilities & Risk Management 24%; Account Opening & Documentation 20%; Supervision & Client Accounts 22%; Complaint Handling & Consequences of Noncompliance 15%) — https://www.csi.ca/en/learning/courses/idsc/exam-credits. The new exam is longer (3 hrs, 90 Qs incl. item sets) and weights account approvals/activity more heavily.

## 4. CONTEXT — merger, why the model changed, mutual-fund side

### Merger
- IIROC + MFDA amalgamated effective January 1, 2023 as the "New Self-Regulatory Organization of Canada"; renamed CIRO on June 1, 2023. Sources: https://www.securities-administrators.ca/new-sro/; https://www.osc.ca/en/industry/market-regulation/self-regulatory-organizations-sro/canadian-investment-regulatory-organization-ciro

### Why the proficiency model changed
- Pre-2026 model was course-centric with a single exclusive provider (CSI). The IIROC–CSI exclusive agreement expired December 31, 2025. CIRO ran a consultation ("Proficiency Assurance – The Next Phase," Bulletin 23-0094, July 7, 2023: https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/proficiency-assurance-next-phase-consultation-relating-expiry-csi-contract), proposed amendments (Bulletin 24-0206, July 4, 2024; 17 comment letters), obtained CSA approval (Bulletin 25-0110, April 17, 2025), effective January 1, 2026. Stated objectives: raise the proficiency bar, increase currency/responsiveness, align with firm training, improve learner experience, lower licensing costs and entry barriers, give CIRO a greater role in designing its own proficiency requirements.
- Delivery partner: Fitch Learning; first official exams launched January 2026 (release Jan 9, 2026: https://finance.yahoo.com/news/ciro-launched-proficiency-model-first-160000980.html). Notably, Fitch Learning also acquired CSI — the old course provider and the new exam administrator are under the same owner. CIRO emphasizes "separation from preparatory courses" as best practice (https://www.investmentexecutive.com/news/industry-news/ciro-says-new-proficiency-models-best-practices-include-separation-from-preparatory-courses/).

### Mutual fund dealer side — Supervisor Exam is investment-dealer-only
- The new proficiency model and the Supervisor Exam apply only to Approved Persons under the IDPC Rules. Mutual fund dealer registrants remain governed by NI 31-103 Part 3 plus the (former MFDA) Mutual Fund Dealer Rules 1.2.3 and 2.5; MFD branch managers — historically supported by CSI's Branch Compliance Officer's Course (BCO) — are not subject to the new Supervisor Exam. Sources: https://nssc.novascotia.ca/CIRO-Delegation; https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/becoming-registered-individual; https://www.csi.ca/en/learning/courses/bco/
- Convergence is coming, not here: under the Rule Consolidation Project, CIRO proposes to regulate approval/experience/proficiency for mutual fund dealer "supervisors (formerly branch managers)" in the consolidated DC Rules — proposed effective Jan 1, 2028. Sources: https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/rule-consolidation-project-proposed-ciro-rules; https://www.blg.com/en/insights/2024/10/ciro-releases-phase-4-of-the-rule-consolidation-project
- One IDPC-side hybrid: RRs/IRs "mutual funds only" under new IDPC s. 2605 — post-approval must complete CIRE + Retail Securities Exam within 270 days and upgrade to RR-securities within 18 months, with 2629 transition options (FAQ #20).
