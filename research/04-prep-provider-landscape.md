# CIRO Supervisor Exam — Third-Party Exam Prep Providers and Study Resources

Research date: June 4, 2026. Context: The CIRO Supervisor Exam is one of 9 proficiency exams that took effect January 1, 2026, replacing CSI's Investment Dealer Supervisors Course (IDSC) pathway. CIRO deliberately runs an "open education" model with no mandatory prep courses and publishes no list of approved providers — it only releases a free syllabus, Guide for Studying, and a practice exam PDF on its Exam Hub (https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/exam-hub/supervisor-exam). Official exams are administered by Fitch Learning; CIRO's exam fees are $475 first attempt / $300 per rewrite, with a 6-month lockout after 3 fails.

- Format: 180 days (6 months) online access; printed manual upgrade +$35 + shipping.
- Price: **$499 CAD early-access (regular $699)**.
- Quality signals: Industry incumbent (long-time CSC/CPH/IDSC prep provider; still sells legacy IDSC prep for transition candidates). Money-back guarantee on CIRO courses, but strict: must register before first attempt, pass SeeWhy's simulation exam, write within subscription, and **fail all three CIRO attempts** (pays out only on the 6-month lockout) — https://seewhylearning.com/guarantee-ciro/. IFSE Institute now refers its LLQP students to SeeWhy. CIRE/RSE/ISE also live; Derivatives in early access; D&E, CCO, CFO, Trader and French courses "Coming: TBA."
- Note: SeeWhy's page claims "110 questions, 150 minutes" for the exam — contradicts official sources (90 Q / 3 h); vendor error.

### 2. ciroexam.ca (Ciroexam) — Supervisor prep LIVE in all-exam subscription
- URLs: https://ciroexam.ca/exams/supervisor and https://ciroexam.ca/pricing
- Status: Live. Dedicated Supervisor course mapped to the official syllabus blueprint (9 elements, 103 learning outcomes); per-element question counts published (e.g., Element 1: 446 Qs; Element 4: 320 Qs) — roughly 1,550+ Supervisor questions total, plus two full-length mocks and a custom weak-area mock.
- Content: 1,400+ questions per course at launch, 16,000+ questions across all 9 exams, 9,000+ confidence-aware flashcards, AI tutor citing the rule on every wrong answer, spaced repetition (SM-2), weakness drills. Element 1 of every course (incl. Supervisor) is free with no card; free 25-question CIRE diagnostic.
- Format: Web + mobile subscription covering all 9 exams.
- Price: **$29.99 CAD/month or $249 CAD/year** (vs $359.88), promo STUDY15 for 15% off first 3 months. 30-day money-back. Explicitly refuses to offer a "pass guarantee."
- Quality signals: Newest entrant ("Made in Canada," independent of CIRO/CSI/Fitch). Reviews page (https://ciroexam.ca/reviews) is self-published "pre-launch candidate interviews" — all for CIRE/RSE, **none for Supervisor** — quality on the Supervisor bank unverified by third parties. Cheapest option by far.

## Market-gap summary

1. **Only 3 live Supervisor products vs ~8 for CIRE.** SeeWhy (premium one-off, $499), ciroexam.ca (cheap subscription, $29.99/mo), Mastery (questions-only app). Everyone else — BCC, FP Canada, Gobeil, Udemy sellers, CourseTree, Smarten Up — went CIRE/RSE-first. The big-volume entry exams got crowded; the role-based exams did not.
2. **No adaptive/guaranteed-pass option for Supervisor.** BCC's ReadyEngine (Level-5 pass guarantee, 97% claimed pass rate) is the strongest consumer proposition in the category but its Supervisor course is still "coming soon." A candidate wanting a pass guarantee today has only SeeWhy's much harsher fail-three-times guarantee.
3. **Even the live products are partially incomplete.** SeeWhy's Supervisor flashcards (June 15, 2026) and proctored simulation (July 15, 2026) post-date today; ciroexam's Supervisor bank has zero published user reviews. First-mover content quality is unproven — amplified by Learnedly's public claim that CIRO's syllabi contain unresolved contradictions.
4. **Underserved niches:** (a) budget one-off purchases — no Udemy/Quizlet/cheap-PDF Supervisor products at all; (b) French-language Supervisor prep — SeeWhy lists French as "Coming: TBA," nobody else mentions it; (c) firm/enterprise Supervisor cohort training — BCC's firm dashboards arrive Q2 2026 but without a Supervisor course; SeeWhy offers corporate pricing but no readiness analytics; (d) instructor-led/live classes — Smarten Up does live classes but skipped Supervisor; (e) community/peer resources — no Reddit threads, no Quizlet decks, no free third-party study notes beyond CIRO's own PDFs and Mastery's free 90-question exam.
5. **Structural tailwind for new entrants:** the two former incumbents for supervisor training are out — CSI/Fitch (conflict: administers the exams) and IFSE (wound down) — and Learnedly opted out, so the Supervisor niche is a 3-horse race with the most credible mainstream brand (BCC) not yet at the start line.
