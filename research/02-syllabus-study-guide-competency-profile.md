## PART 1 — SUPERVISOR EXAM SYLLABUS (complete topic structure + weightings)

Source: official syllabus PDF, "Supervisor Exam Syllabus, Last updated May 29, 2025" (EN: ciro.ca/media/13606; FR: ocri.ca/media/13606, doc DC-18822). English PDF would not extract; the structure below is the complete official outline reconstructed from the **full official French edition (retrieved verbatim)** cross-checked against **Google-indexed English text of the official PDF** (English headings/summaries below that are verbatim-confirmed are noted). Outcome-level wording for 1.1–9.3 is a faithful translation of the official French.

### 1.A Exam parameters (verbatim-confirmed in English)
- Papers: **1 paper**
- Exam format: **Proctored (remote or in person)**
- Exam length: **3 hours**
- Question format: Multiple choice — **60 standard questions + 30 item-set questions**
- Number of questions per exam: **90**
- Number of attempts allowed per exam: **3**

### 1.B Question weighting (verbatim English table, confirmed via indexed PDF)

| 3 Business and operations | 9 |

| 7 Trading and market rules | 6 |
| 8 Advertisements, sales literature and communications and research reports | 7 |

| **Total** | **90** |

### 1.C Explanation of the syllabus (taxonomy)
"The syllabus is divided into a series of learning outcomes. Each learning outcome expects the candidate to remember, understand, apply or analyze information" (verbatim-confirmed English). Four cognitive levels, each with related verbs and sample question styles:
- **Remember** — recall facts, rules, principles (describe, identify, recognize, state)
- **Understand** — demonstrate comprehension of a problem, fact, rule or principle (describe, explain, interpret, recognize, select)
- **Apply** — use information in specific situations (calculate, demonstrate, implement, use)
- **Analyze** — examine information and draw conclusions or make connections (compare, contrast, distinguish, examine)

Note (from syllabus): the levels are cumulative — e.g., "Apply"-level outcomes may also be assessed at the "Understand" and "Remember" levels.

### 1.D Element-by-element outline (all learning outcomes)

#### ELEMENT 1: General regulatory framework (10 questions)
**Summary (verbatim-confirmed English):** "This element starts with an understanding of the regulators in the Canadian investment services [sector] and the protections afforded to consumers through legislation, such as the Criminal Code and the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorism Financing Act. Element 1 also requires the Supervisor to apply procedures to client complaints handling and critically analyze conduct that could breach the Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO) standards of conduct and conflicts of interest rules."

- **1.1 Understand** the role and authority of the Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) and provincial/territorial securities and derivatives regulators, considering: CSA jurisdiction; CSA mandate and objectives; purpose and impact of the different forms of legislation — National Instruments, Multilateral Instruments (MI), National Policies, Staff Notices, Companion Policies; Investment Dealer and individual registration requirements; enforcement powers.
- **1.2 Understand** the role and authority of CIRO, considering: CIRO jurisdiction; purpose of recognition orders and delegated authorities; CIRO mandate and objectives; dealer registration and individual approval requirements; Investment Dealer and Partially Consolidated Rules (IDPC Rules); Universal Market Integrity Rules (UMIR); role and implications of rules, guidance notes, forms and supporting schedules, including standards of conduct, sales practices, external communications; CIRO enforcement powers.
- **1.3 Understand** the registration requirements for Investment Dealers and approval of individuals, and the role of the CSA and CIRO in that process.
- **1.4 Understand** the role and authority of exchanges and other marketplaces, considering: exchanges; Alternative Trading Systems (ATS); crypto-asset trading platforms; Foreign Organized Regulated Markets.
- **1.5 Understand** the role and authority of clearing houses, considering: The Canadian Depository for Securities Limited (CDS); Canadian Derivatives Clearing Corporation (CDCC).
- **1.6 Understand** the role of the Canadian Investor Protection Fund (CIPF): purpose and objective; funding requirements imposed on Investment Dealers; governance structure; role of CIPF in a dealer's bankruptcy/insolvency, including pooling of client assets.
- **1.7 Understand** the function and purpose of other financial services regulators and agencies, considering: Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario (FSRA); Bank of Canada; RCMP Integrated Market Enforcement Teams (IMET); Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada (FINTRAC); Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI); federal and provincial privacy commissioners; Ombudsman for Banking Services and Investments (OBSI); US and other foreign securities and derivatives regulators — United States: Securities Exchange Commission (SEC), Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA), Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC), National Futures Association (NFA).
- **1.8 Understand** the purpose and implications of the following federal statutes for the investment sector: Bank Act (ensuring stability and integrity; ensuring consumer protection; promoting competition); Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act, Part XII – Bankruptcy of a Securities Firm.
- **1.9 Understand** the purpose and implications of the Criminal Code and its application to financial crimes, considering: fraud; theft; theft by a person holding power of attorney; misappropriation of money held under direction; false statement or false pretenses; obtaining execution of a security by fraud; forgery; uttering a forged document; false prospectus; organized crime offences; insider trading.
- **1.10 Understand** the purpose and implications of the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorism Financing Act (PCMLTFA) and its Regulations, including: stages of money laundering; compliance program requirements; anti-money-laundering policies and procedures; client information and client due diligence requirements; enterprise risk assessment and risk indicators; employee training; business relationship record-keeping.
- **1.11 Understand** the purpose and implications of other applicable laws, considering: Canadian Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL) — reduce spam, improve privacy protection, promote e-commerce, deter malicious activity, encourage compliance and accountability; confidentiality/non-disclosure agreements — protection of proprietary information, legal recourse, fostering business relationships, maintaining competitive advantage; Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) — protection of personal information, fair information handling principles; rules for public company disclosure and statutory rights of shareholders.
- **1.12 Understand** the Investment Dealer's obligations to clients and others, considering: legislative; contractual; organizational factors.
- **1.13 Apply** client complaint requirements to specific situations relevant to Supervisors, considering: reporting requirements and failure to report; obligation to conduct an internal investigation; settlement agreements; complaint policies and procedures (client-related or otherwise); role and requirements of CIRO, OBSI and provincial/territorial regulators; role of a designated complaints officer for client complaints; alternative complaint resolution processes.
- **1.14 Understand** the recourse available to dissatisfied clients, including arbitration and litigation, OBSI and CIRO.
- **1.15 Understand** situations that may require use of CIRO's whistleblower service for non-client account matters, including: misconduct or illegal activity within an organization; potential systemic wrongdoing; potential securities frauds.
- **1.16 Understand** the consequences and liabilities of settlement agreements entered into with clients without the Investment Dealer's approval, and penalties regulators may apply.
- **1.17 Analyze** situations involving the role of ethics and personal integrity of Supervisors in the securities industry, considering: the link between ethical behaviour and compliance (due care; independent professional judgment; trustworthiness and integrity; honesty and fairness; professionalism); CIRO standards of conduct (high standards of ethics and conduct; acting openly and fairly; not engaging in conduct unbecoming or detrimental to the public interest; acting in accordance with just and equitable principles of trade; conduct that could breach the standards of conduct).
- **1.18 Analyze** situations involving the role of ethics and integrity of Supervisors in corporate governance, considering: transparency; resolution of compensation conflicts; oversight and accountability; training and awareness.
- **1.19 Understand** the consequences and risks of unethical behaviour, considering: legal consequences; reputational damage; regulatory scrutiny; employee morale and turnover; client trust and retention.
- **1.20 Apply** CIRO conflicts-of-interest requirements to specific situations, considering: obligations to avoid, disclose, manage and resolve material conflicts; applicable standards of care, ethics and independent supervision for producing Supervisors; identification of potential and existing conflicts, including compensatory, managerial, ownership; protection and proper use of corporate assets and opportunities; confidentiality of corporate, client and third-party information; fair dealing with security holders, clients, suppliers, competitors and employees.
- **1.21 Analyze** situations that could lead to the prohibition, approval, disclosure and required action regarding outside activities and positions of influence, including: existing and reasonably foreseeable material conflicts of interest; written disclosure where applicable; reporting obligations where applicable.
- **1.22 Apply** the requirements relating to prohibitions and exemptions on personal financial dealings with clients to specific situations, considering: acceptance of consideration; settlement agreements; borrowing from clients; lending to clients; control or authority.
- **1.23 Understand** the processes used to contain confidential or material non-public information, including: persons likely to receive confidential information (investment banking and corporate finance personnel; other employees who may access confidential information, e.g., issuer trading operations, research departments, relationships with company insiders); information barriers and firewalls; grey lists and restricted lists; cybersecurity.

#### ELEMENT 2: Supervisory structure: Investment Dealer responsibilities (10 questions)
**Summary (verbatim-confirmed English):** "Element 2 requires the Supervisor to understand the obligations of an Investment Dealer when providing products and services to clients, including the role of Executives and the compliance department. Supervisors will also be required to analyze and apply to specific situations their general responsibilities, including the role of delegation [and automation] in supervision."

- **2.1 Understand** the requirement for an Investment Dealer to establish a supervision system to supervise the activities of all of its employees and Approved Persons to ensure compliance with CIRO rules and securities laws. The supervision system must, at minimum: include policies and procedures designed to supervise employees and Approved Persons; include policies and procedures providing reasonable assurance of compliance with CIRO requirements, securities laws and applicable laws; ensure all supervisory policies and procedures are written and amended within a reasonable time after changes to CIRO rules or securities laws; provide for communication of policies and procedures to all relevant employees and Approved Persons, and must: provide sales-practice policies and procedures applicable to their functions; provide for receipt of acknowledgment that policies have been read and understood; provide introductory and continuing training about the dealer's policies and procedures and relevant changes; communicate information regarding CIRO rules and applicable laws to all sales employees and other Approved Persons; include policies and procedures addressing the method and timing of distributing compliance-related notices; promptly communicate changes to all relevant employees and Approved Persons; include procedures providing reasonable assurance that each employee and Approved Person understands their responsibilities under the policies and procedures; provide for annual review of financial and operational policies and procedures; ensure Supervisors understand the dealer's record-keeping obligation.
- **2.2 Understand** product due diligence obligations and exemptions, including: applicable policies and procedures; assessment of relevant aspects of products the dealer makes available; approval of products to be made available to clients; monitoring the products made available; exemptions for accounts held by carrying brokers or trade-execution/clearing/settlement/custody-only dealers.
- **2.3 Understand** the responsibility of Executives in supervising the dealer's activities, considering: CIRO requirements to designate relevant Executives; oversight of relevant policies and procedures; supervising and directing the dealer's activities, employees and Approved Persons to provide reasonable assurance of compliance with CIRO requirements and securities laws; accountability to Directors for establishing a supervision system; reporting requirements of the Chief Compliance Officer and Chief Financial Officer to the Board.
- **2.4 Understand** an Executive's responsibility for significant areas of risk, considering: financial; operational; corporate finance; trading; wealth management; research.
- **2.5 Understand** the implications of internal and external audits on the Investment Dealer.
- **2.6 Analyze** situations reflecting common areas of regulatory action (including enforcement) by CIRO against one or more of a dealer's Approved Persons, considering: violating securities and derivatives laws, regulations or CIRO requirements; aiding and abetting another in such violations; failing to satisfy applicable supervisory obligations.
- **2.7 Understand** the role of the compliance function, including: setting policies and procedures to ensure compliance with applicable requirements; identifying, assessing, advising on, acting on, communicating, monitoring, escalating and reporting on the dealer's compliance with regulatory requirements and its own policies and procedures; CIRO's expectations of a dealer's compliance functions and the role, responsibility and accountability of all dealer personnel; escalation responsibility.
- **2.8 Analyze** the scope of supervision requirements and the Supervisor's authority to act on the dealer's behalf, considering: providing reasonable assurance of compliance with CIRO requirements, securities and derivatives laws and regulations and other applicable laws; supervising employees and Approved Persons in accordance with assigned supervisory responsibilities (operation of account types; supervision of shared office premises; supervision of sales practices; method, timing and distribution of compliance-related notices) and the dealer's policies and procedures; the requirement for Supervisors' authority; situations where client records may be amended, and controls over accessing and amending client records.
- **2.9 Apply** record-keeping requirements and record-retention periods to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, considering: list of Supervisors, applicable qualifications, specific responsibilities and dates of designation; supervisory reports; identification of compliance issues; terms of delegation and authority for Supervisors.
- **2.10 Understand** documented controls for delegation and **apply** them to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, considering: detailed policies and procedures laying out how delegation works and to whom tasks/activities are delegated; maintaining records of delegated tasks; restrictions on delegation (delegate qualification; prohibitions on delegation; delegator's retained responsibility); processes for escalating significant issues to the delegator; list of factors that trigger a review by the delegator; examination of the delegate's reviews to ensure adherence to applicable policies and procedures and that items recommended for approval would have been approved by the delegator; regular reports from delegate to delegator; regular (at minimum annual) reviews of the delegation process, with policy revisions as needed.
- **2.11 Understand** the role of automation, considering: how the automation process works; the Supervisor's role and responsibilities; tasks and activities that can and cannot be automated.
- **2.12 Understand** the dealer's controls, policies and procedures for automation processes, considering: factors that can trigger a manual review — applicant not being an individual; applicant not having reached the age of majority; trading authorization being used; applicant applying to use derivatives trading strategies without an appropriate level of knowledge and trading experience; applicant being an insider; applicant being a politically exposed person (PEP); appropriate periodic testing and auditing to ensure the automated process functions as designed.

#### ELEMENT 3: Business and operations (9 questions)
(Full French body title: "Responsabilités de surveillance propres aux activités et opérations des courtiers en placement" = Specific supervision responsibilities in relation to the Investment Dealer's business and operations.)
**Summary (verbatim-confirmed English):** "In this section the Supervisor needs to understand how an Investment Dealer's supervisory structure can change depending on its business model. Element 3 also requires the Supervisor to analyze supervisory risks associated with different account, products and compensation structures."

- **3.1 Understand** the dealer's responsibility to implement a supervision system adapted to the scope and complexity of the business, considering: policies and procedures specific to the dealer's supervision systems (the Supervisor's responsibility to consider their effectiveness); the responsibility to appoint as many Supervisors as needed to properly supervise the activities of employees and Approved Persons (the need for Supervisors to escalate concerns that could result in non-compliance or the inability to supervise properly); the dealer's business model, including: types of products traded and offered; type of trading conducted by the dealer and its Approved Persons; due diligence procedures on new products and services and ongoing risk assessment of existing products and services; location of the dealer's Supervisors and other Approved Persons; different types of Supervisors and their respective functions and responsibilities.
- **3.2 Understand** the relationship between a dealer's supervisory system and its internal controls.
- **3.3 Understand** [CORRECTED against English PDF; French-based draft said "Analyze"] the risks, opportunities and requirements for the Investment Dealer associated with each of the following business models: advisory (including role and responsibilities of Approved Persons; working arrangements; principal-agent and employer/employee); portfolio management; managed account model (including role and responsibilities of Portfolio Managers and Associate Portfolio Managers); online advice (operations, regulatory obligations and product limitations; product limits such as exchange-traded funds (ETFs); role and responsibilities of Approved Persons); order-execution-only (OEO) model (role and responsibilities of Approved Persons; tools used at the dealer and available to clients; Direct Electronic Access (DEA); contracts for difference (CFDs)).
- **3.4 Understand** the supervisory risks associated with each of the following client types: retail; institutional.
- **3.5 Analyze** the supervisory risks associated with each of the following accounts for both retail and institutional clients, as applicable, considering: advisory accounts; in-house managed accounts; third-party managed accounts; discretionary accounts; fee-based (non-managed)/wrap accounts; tax-deferred accounts; margin accounts; Direct Electronic Access (DEA); Delivery Against Payment (DAP); Cash On Delivery (COD); Receive Against Payment (RAP).
- **3.6 Analyze** the supervisory risks associated with each of the following types of securities: equities; mutual funds and ETFs; fixed income products.
- **3.7 Analyze** the supervisory risks associated with each of the following complex securities/product types: leveraged and inverse ETFs; structured products; asset-backed securities; specialized securities (e.g., cryptocurrencies).
- **3.8 Analyze** the supervisory risks associated with each of the following types of derivatives: options; futures contract options; futures; forwards; swaps; contracts for difference; other similar derivatives as described by CIRO.
- **3.9 Analyze** the supervisory risks associated with each of the following compensation structures, considering: commission-based; fee-based.
- **3.10 Understand** the supervisory risks associated with negotiated flat fees and referral fees.

#### ELEMENT 4: Account approvals (20 questions — the most heavily weighted element)
**Summary (verbatim-confirmed English, partial):** "Element 4 requires the Supervisor to understand the requirements for account approvals for a range of accounts," including order-execution-only (OEO) accounts, discretionary accounts, managed accounts and derivatives accounts, and to apply many of these requirements to specific account-opening situations.

- **4.1 Understand** account approval requirements, considering: collection of client account documentation; prescribed timelines; business-day approval requirements; updates and changes; handling of conflicts of interest; non-client account requirements.
- **4.2 Understand** account approval considerations for institutional clients: definition of an institutional client; circumstances where a qualifying hedger can be classified as an institutional client; the determination undertaken to classify an institutional client as a hedger; ensuring the client qualifies as an institutional client; supervision requirements for institutional clients not exempted from suitability obligations.
- **4.3 Understand** account appropriateness requirements, including: putting prospective clients' interests first; appropriate scope and selection of products, services and account relationships to which clients have access.
- **4.4 Understand** client account record requirements, including: documentation supporting the conclusion that the client's identity has been verified; documentation supporting the account appropriateness assessment; KYC information collected in accordance with CIRO requirements; the client's account application.
- **4.5 Understand** the purpose and content of the Relationship Disclosure, including: products, services and account types the client can access at the dealer, including fee-based, leverage and margin accounts; limitations of products, services and account types offered, where applicable; fees and guidelines for compensation and relevant costs; conditions under which the client's account will be maintained; the wealth management process and its impact on investment decisions; investment performance benchmarks; impact of fees, turnover and taxes on managed product returns; the client relationship model; systematic approaches to investment management.
- **4.6 Understand** the leverage risk disclosure statement, considering: when it is required; its purpose; its content.
- **4.7 Understand** the purpose and content of the margin account agreement, considering: client's obligation to repay indebtedness and maintain adequate margin; client's obligation to pay interest on debit balances; dealer's right to raise money on and pledge assets in the client's account; extent of the dealer's right to use free credit balances for its own business or to cover debits in the same or other accounts; dealer's right to sell assets in the account and make purchases to cover short sales; nature of prior notice, where applicable, and the client's obligations to remedy any deficiency; extent of the dealer's right, if any, to use a security in the client's account for delivery against a short sale; extent of the dealer's right, if any, to use a security in the client's account for delivery against a short sale in an account owned or controlled by the dealer, a partner or a Director; extent of the dealer's right to use assets in the account and hold them as collateral for the client's debt; dealer's obligation to carry out all transactions in accordance with CIRO requirements and, where applicable, the requirements of the marketplace on which the transaction is executed.
- **4.8 Understand** the dealer-related disclosures in the derivatives trading agreement, considering: time periods during which the dealer accepts orders for execution; dealer's rights to — exercise discretion in accepting orders; impose trading or position limits or close out positions under specified conditions; use client free credit balances within its own business or to cover debits in the same or other accounts; use client account assets as collateral for the client's debit and position obligations; raise money on and pledge assets held in the account; conditions under which the dealer may apply the client's funds, securities or other property in the same or other client accounts to satisfy outstanding debts or margin calls; dealer's obligations to — provide information to regulators regarding position limits, exercise limit requirements and reporting of derivatives positions/transaction data; obtain client consent before the dealer may take the other side of the client's transaction and document that consent; address errors and omissions; if discretionary authority is granted to the dealer, it must be clearly explained and specifically confirmed by the client unless provided in another document consistent with applicable requirements.
- **4.9 Understand** the client-related disclosures in the derivatives trading agreement, considering: client obligations to — comply with CIRO requirements and the requirements of any entity through which the derivative is traded, cleared or issued, including reporting and prescribed position and exercise limits; maintain adequate margin collateral and pay debts owed to the dealer; pay commission or other compensation, if any; pay interest, if any, on debit balances; client acknowledgments of — receiving the most recent derivatives risk disclosure statement; their obligation to inform/update the dealer of circumstances under which they would be considered an insider of a reporting issuer or any other issuer whose securities are publicly traded.
- **4.10 Understand** other requirements of an entity through which a derivative is traded, cleared or issued, considering: the dealer's deadlines for a client to submit an exercise notice (and the method used to distribute assignment notices); written notice that — the dealer may set maximum limits on short positions; the dealer may apply cash-only terms during the last 10 days before expiry; CIRO may impose other rules affecting existing or subsequent transactions; the client's obligation to instruct the dealer to close out positions before expiry; a statement that the dealer requires the client to maintain minimum margin that is the greater of — the amount prescribed by the derivatives marketplace or clearing house, CIRO's requirements, or the dealer's requirements.
- **4.11 Understand** client cumulative loss limits: applies to an account where transactions involve futures contracts, forward contracts, CFDs, futures contract options or similar derivatives, or highly leveraged securities or derivatives; applies to an account other than a hedging account, whether advisory, discretionary, managed or order-execution-only; limits must be determined on — a lifetime basis and validated with the client annually, or an annual basis and updated annually.
- **4.12 Understand** the purpose and content of a Letter of Undertaking as an alternative to a trading agreement, for institutional clients only.
- **4.13 Understand** the requirements for the supervision of managed accounts, considering the need to: designate a Supervisor responsible for managed accounts; establish policies and procedures specifically addressing the supervision and operation of managed accounts in accordance with applicable requirements; enter into a managed account agreement with the client prior to opening; approve each managed account in writing; retain a record of the approval; provide the client with a copy of its policy ensuring fair allocation of investment opportunities.
- **4.14 Understand** the purpose and content of the managed account agreement, considering: description of or reference to the client's — financial situation; investment knowledge; risk profile; investment objectives; time horizon; description of any investment restrictions imposed by the client, where permitted by the dealer; terms of termination in accordance with applicable requirements; termination by written notice — by the client (effective on receipt by the dealer, except for transactions entered prior to receipt) or by the dealer (effective not less than 30 days from delivery of the notice to the client).
- **4.15 Apply** managed-account conflict-of-interest rules to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, including restrictions on: the dealer trading on its own account, or knowingly permitting or arranging for any associate or affiliate to trade, in reliance on information relating to trades made or to be made in a managed account; knowingly allowing, without the client's prior written consent, a managed account to invest in — a security or derivative of a security of an issuer related or connected to a responsible person or the dealer, or where the individual specifically authorized to deal with managed accounts is an officer or Director of the issuer (unless the relationship is disclosed to the client); new issues or secondary offerings underwritten by the dealer; knowingly causing any managed account to — purchase or sell a security or derivative of a security from or to the account of a Portfolio Manager, an Associate Portfolio Manager or an associate of either, or an investment fund for which a responsible person acts as adviser; provide a guarantee or loan to a responsible person or an associate of a responsible person; unfair allocation of investment opportunities among managed accounts.
- **4.16 Understand** fees and remuneration rules and prohibitions on certain types of fees and compensation, including: the dealer must not compensate a person based on the value or volume of transactions in the account; the dealer may not charge a client directly, without a written agreement, for services rendered to the managed account based on volume or value of transactions in the account, or contingent upon profit or performance of the client's account.
- **4.17 Understand** that a designated Supervisor is prohibited from delegating the performance of discretionary account reviews.
- **4.18 Understand** the requirements for the supervision of discretionary accounts, considering the need to: designate one or more Supervisors who meet applicable proficiency requirements to be responsible for discretionary accounts; establish policies and procedures specifically addressing supervision and operation of discretionary accounts; identify discretionary accounts in its books and records to permit supervision in accordance with applicable requirements; enter into a discretionary account agreement with the client prior to accepting the account as discretionary; designate a Supervisor who must approve the account as a discretionary account and approve the signed discretionary account agreement; maintain a record of the designated Supervisor's approval in accordance with record retention requirements.
- **4.19 Understand** the purpose and content of a discretionary account agreement, the required disclosures, rights and obligations where permitted by the dealer, including: defining the extent of the discretionary authority given to the dealer by the client; restrictions on the discretionary authority; maximum term of no longer than 12 months (non-renewable); termination terms in accordance with applicable requirements; termination by written notice only (client — effective on receipt by the dealer, except for orders entered prior to receipt; dealer — effective not less than 30 days from delivery of the notice); specific conflict-of-interest rules for discretionary accounts, including that a discretionary account must not hold any publicly traded securities of the dealer or its affiliates; the responsible person/dealer must not — trade for their own account based on discretionary account trade information; provide guarantees; permit trading by related/interested parties.
- **4.20 Apply** the account appropriateness assessment to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor for: advisory accounts; managed accounts; discretionary accounts; leveraged accounts; margin accounts; derivatives accounts, including use of derivatives trading strategies.
- **4.21 Apply** the account appropriateness assessment to OEO accounts in specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, considering: whether a person would be incapable of conducting online activities (e.g., clear and persistent difficulties completing the OEO dealer's online new-account application form); whether the investor is looking for advice; restrictions on automated derivatives account approvals for OEO dealers, including — trading in CFDs; OTC derivatives; futures or similar derivatives trading strategies; options or similar derivatives trading strategies other than low-risk strategies based on long calls, long puts, covered calls, or protective (long) puts.

#### ELEMENT 5: Account activity (15 questions)
**Summary (verbatim-confirmed English):** "Element 5 continues the theme of Element 4 but now requires an understanding of the ongoing risks of different accounts. It requires the Supervisor to analyze situations to identify what risks may be present and apply appropriate procedures to mitigate or respond to those risks."

- **5.1 Understand** the different approaches to supervision, including: tiered approach; single centralized approach; considerations relevant to remote supervision.
- **5.2 Apply** account supervision requirements to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, considering: identification of clients presenting a high risk to the dealer; identification of clients presenting a high risk of improper activities in securities markets; compliance with all anti-money laundering and terrorist financing requirements under applicable laws and regulations; inclusion of controls for accessing and amending client records.
- **5.3 Understand** the strict supervisory measures applicable to Approved Persons with a history of regulatory infractions or questionable conduct: pre-approval of orders by the Supervisor; daily and monthly review of all client accounts.
- **5.4 Understand** the risks associated with the method of order entry and the absence of intermediation by dealer employees (where approved for OEO accounts).
- **5.5 Understand** the requirements and conditions for applying temporary holds to prevent financial exploitation of vulnerable clients.
- **5.6 Analyze** situations relevant to a Supervisor concerning retail client account supervision, considering: daily supervision of trading activity, including review of red flags and trades that may not comply with CIRO requirements and applicable policies and procedures; monthly supervision of trading activity, including review of specified account types; detection of concerning and suspicious account practices, such as — unsuitable trading; undue concentration of securities, derivatives or precious metals bullion in a single account or across accounts; excessive trading; trading in restricted securities or derivatives whose underlying interest is a restricted security; conflict of interest between the client-facing Approved Person and the client's trading activity; excessive transaction transfers and trade cancellations indicating possible unauthorized trading; inappropriate or high-risk trading strategies; deterioration of the quality of client holdings; excessive or improper crosses of securities, derivatives or precious metals bullion between clients; improper or excessive employee trading; front running; account number changes; late payments; outstanding margin calls; undisclosed short sales; manipulative and deceptive activities; insider trading; transactional information required for retail client instructions, including direct, indirect and subsequent client charges or commissions.
- **5.7 Understand** cross-supervision requirements.
- **5.8 Understand** client mail-hold account procedures for retail clients, considering: authorization received from the client; time limits; control and regular review.
- **5.9 Apply** the requirements in policies and procedures that specifically address the following, to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor: supervision and review of trading activity in institutional client accounts; actions to deal with problems or issues identified through supervisory review; detection of improper or suspicious account activity, including — manipulative and deceptive activities; trading in securities on the dealer's restricted list; trading in derivatives whose underlying interest is on the dealer's restricted list; front running in employee or proprietary accounts; trading in securities with restrictions on transfer; transactions in derivatives whose underlying interest has restrictions on transfer; exceeding derivatives position or exercise limits; trading activity around margin/reduced-margin rates and price thresholds.
- **5.10 Understand** direct market access account and supervision requirements for institutional clients, including with respect to algorithmic trading.
- **5.11 Understand** OEO account services exemptions and requirements, considering: policies and procedures for reviewing client trading; ongoing supervisory requirements for automated OEO account approvals; risks associated with the direct method of order entry (e.g., no Approved Person intermediary); applicable CFD undertakings.
- **5.12 Understand** CIRO requirements for handling derivatives accounts, including the need to designate one or more alternate Supervisors where continuous supervision of derivatives trading activity is required.
- **5.13 Apply** requirements specific to retail clients with derivatives accounts, to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, to detect: excessive intra-day and short-term transactions; trading while under-margined; trading beyond margin or credit limits; exceeding approved loss limits; exceeding derivatives position or exercise limits; speculative transactions in hedging accounts; trading in derivatives whose underlying interest is on the dealer's restricted list; transactions in derivatives whose underlying interest has restrictions on transfer; trading derivatives to avoid insider trading restrictions; exposures from uncovered option positions; exposures to delivery obligations from holding futures contracts, forward contracts or similar derivatives into the delivery month.
- **5.14 Apply** policies and procedures to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, considering: notifying clients of — approaching expiry dates; significant changes in derivatives resulting from changes in the underlying interest; changes in the dealer's business policy; new developments in the trading or regulation of derivatives that may impact clients; requiring approval to solicit clients to use derivatives; requiring Supervisor approval to solicit clients to use derivatives and derivatives programs; preventing a client from transacting in derivatives without an executed derivatives trading agreement; arranging handling of futures contracts, forward contracts and similar derivatives with pending delivery months; detecting derivatives trading by a client who is an insider of a reporting issuer or any other issuer whose securities are publicly traded, intended to avoid insider trading restrictions; preventing a retail client from holding CFD or similar derivatives positions representing more than 0.5% of the float of a reporting issuer or any other publicly traded issuer on an intra-day or short-term basis; prohibiting the offering to retail clients of CFDs or similar derivatives that confer the right or obligation to acquire or deliver the underlying interest or confer any other shareholder rights, such as voting rights; directing clients to qualified Approved Persons during normal business hours.
- **5.15 Apply** policies and procedures that specifically address the supervision of individuals responsible for handling managed accounts to specific situations relevant to a Supervisor, considering: managed-account conflict-of-interest requirements; fairness in the allocation of investment opportunities; providing reasonable assurance of compliance with CIRO requirements, securities and derivatives laws and regulations and other applicable laws; direct supervision of Associate Portfolio Managers, including applicable conditions and exceptions; requirements and responsibilities of a managed account committee.
- **5.16 Understand** the importance for Approved Persons, including Supervisors, of preparing and retaining relevant documentation and assessments of clients and client activity in relation to the above activities.

## PART 4 — REFERENCE DOCUMENT FOR SUPERVISOR COMPETENCIES (Appendix 16) — complete structure with full sub-competency content
Source (retrieved in full, 59 pages / 97K chars): https://www.ciro.ca/media/13631/download?attachment — "APPENDIX 16 — Reference Document for: Supervisor Competencies. Refer to Appendix 8 to review the Supervisor competency profile."

Framing statement (verbatim): "From a regulatory perspective, the competencies for a highly competent and compliant Supervisor at a minimum include the following:" Three competencies (matching Appendix 8): **1. General Regulatory Framework (p.3)**, **2. Supervisory Structure: Dealer Responsibilities (p.10)**, **3. Supervisory Structure: Specific Supervision Responsibilities (p.17)**. Each sub-competency is split into two columns: **"KNOWLEDGE — For Supervisors to understand as applicable"** and **"BEHAVIOURS AND SKILLS — For Supervisors to apply as applicable."**

### Competency 1: General Regulatory Framework
**Sub-competency I. Role of securities and derivatives regulators, SRO and applicable laws**
- Knowledge: securities and derivatives regulators, marketplaces/exchanges and SRO, including: CSA and provincial/territorial securities and derivatives regulators (jurisdiction; mandate and objectives; governance structure; legislation; NIs, MIs, National Policies, Staff Notices and Companion Policies; distinctions between provincial regulatory frameworks; enforcement powers; dealer and individual registration requirements); role and authority of marketplaces (exchanges; ATSs; Crypto-Asset Trading Platforms (CTPs); Foreign Organized Regulated Markets (FORM)); CIRO (jurisdiction; recognition orders and delegated authorities; mandate and objectives; enforcement powers; dealer registration and individual approval requirements; IDPC Rules; UMIR; role and implications of rules, guidance notes, forms and supporting schedules, including standards of conduct, sales practices, external communications).
- Behaviours/skills: act in accordance with requirements applicable to the approved role; keep up to date with relevant business, industry, legal and regulatory developments; support policies and procedures implementation/updates; communicate relevant updates to Approved Persons, employees and others; support and promote sources of information; support implementation and maintenance of an internal monitoring system to identify changes and impacts on the dealer.

**Sub-competency II. Role of other financial services regulators and applicable laws**
- Knowledge: other regulators and agencies, including clearing agencies (CDS; CDCC); CIPF (purpose and objective; funding requirements; governance structure; role in dealer bankruptcy/insolvency including pooling of customer assets); FSRA; Bank of Canada; RCMP IMET; FINTRAC; OSFI; federal and provincial privacy commissioners; OBSI; US and other foreign securities and derivatives regulators. Other applicable laws: federal statutes (Bank Act; Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act Part XII – Bankruptcy of a Securities Firm); Criminal Code financial crimes (fraud; theft; theft by person holding power of attorney; misappropriation of money under direction; false statement or false pretenses; obtaining execution of security by fraud; forgery; uttering a forged document; false prospectus; organized crime offences; illegal insider trading); confidentiality agreements; privacy legislation (PIPEDA); CASL; PCMLTFA and Regulations (compliance program requirements; AML policies and procedures; client information and due diligence requirements; enterprise risk assessment and indicators; employee training; business relationship record-keeping); rules for public company disclosure and statutory rights of shareholders.
- Behaviours/skills: act in accordance with applicable legal requirements; keep current; support policies and procedures implementation/updates; support internal monitoring system.

**Sub-competency III. Internal investigation and client complaint handling**
- Knowledge: client complaint requirements; complaints policies and procedures (client-related or otherwise); role and requirements of CIRO, OBSI and provincial/territorial regulators; role of a designated complaints officer; CIRO whistleblower service for non-client account matters (misconduct or illegal activity within an organization; potential systemic wrongdoing; potential securities frauds); alternative complaint resolution processes; penalties regulators can apply; obligations to clients and others (legislative; contractual; organizational); recourse for dissatisfied clients, including arbitration and litigation; consequences and liabilities of improper dealings with clients.
- Behaviours/skills: keep updated on retail and institutional complaints against the dealer, Approved Persons or employees/agents; follow procedures to inform Executives of allegations of serious misconduct; assist with client complaints filed with the dealer's compliance department; support oversight of complaint handling, litigation and other regulatory reviews; support implementation of policies and procedures for identification, record-keeping and reporting of external client litigation, OBSI and other alternatives, and CIRO investigations.

**Sub-competency IV. Ethics, conflicts of interest and confidentiality**
- Knowledge: CIRO standards of conduct; ethics in the securities industry (proper care; independent professional judgment; trustworthiness and integrity; honesty and fairness; professionalism); ethics in corporate governance, including compensation conflicts; consequences and risks of unethical behaviour; types of ethical dilemmas; conflicts of interest rules and obligations; applicable standards of care, ethics and independent supervision for producing Supervisors; potential and existing conflicts (compensatory; managerial; ownership); protection and proper use of corporate assets and opportunities; confidentiality of corporate, client and third-party information; fair dealing with securities holders, clients, suppliers, competitors and employees; prohibition, approval, disclosure and action requirements regarding outside activities and positions of influence (existing and reasonably foreseeable material conflicts; written disclosure; reporting obligations); prohibition and exemptions regarding personal financial dealings with clients; containment of confidential information (information barriers and firewalls; grey and restricted lists; role of investment banking and corporate finance; role of research department; cybersecurity).
- Behaviours/skills: comply with CIRO and other applicable ethical standards of conduct; apply an ethical decision-making process (identify the issue; identify the individuals involved; gather the facts and identify potential courses of action and their consequences; make the decision; reflect on the process); ensure conflicts are addressed, disclosed and/or avoided per applicable rules, including personal financial dealings; consider policies and procedures when handling material non-public information in the necessary course of business; identify and resolve personnel non-compliance with conflicts rules; monitor Approved Persons and employees for adherence to requirements, policies and procedures, and legislation; escalate to the appropriate Executive or compliance personnel.

### Competency 2: Supervisory Structure: Dealer Responsibilities
**Sub-competency I. General dealer responsibilities**
- Knowledge: supervision and compliance rules and other dealer requirements, including applicable policies and procedures; record-keeping rules and requirements; annual supervisory review of financial and operational policies and procedures; KYC obligations, requirements and exemptions; product due diligence obligations and exemptions (applicable policies and procedures; assessment of relevant aspects of products; approval of products; monitoring the products; exemptions for accounts held by carrying brokers and trade-execution/clearing/settlement/custody-only dealers).
- Behaviours/skills: keep complete and accurate records for an audit trail; monitor and report on adequacy of supervisory resources at assigned business locations; implement compliance-function recommendations; ensure issues identified are resolved and recorded; communicate and escalate non-compliance and red flags; assist with the annual supervisory review; ensure access restrictions to dealer systems on client-facing Approved Persons and other employees to prevent material client information changes without required approvals; ensure controls to identify red flags for informal trading authorizations (e.g., unrelated individuals appointed as Power of Attorney); ensure supervision responsibilities/requirements are specific to non-client accounts, discretionary accounts, managed accounts, registered accounts and restricted accounts.

**Sub-competency II. Oversight by Executives**
- Knowledge: Executives' responsibility to comply with CIRO requirements, securities and derivatives laws, regulations and other applicable laws considering the scope and complexity of the dealer's business; their role in supervising and directing the dealer's activities, employees and Approved Persons; accountability to Directors for establishing a supervisory system; CIRO requirements to designate appropriate Executives; Executives' role in reviewing account supervision policies and procedures, including substantive amendments; risk management in a principles-based regulatory environment as it applies to supervision; responsibility for significant areas of risk (financial; operational; corporate finance; trading; wealth management; research); Director and Executive responsibilities to supervise and direct the dealer's activities; roles and responsibilities of Executives, including CCO and CFO reporting requirements to the Board; implications of internal and external audits; common areas of CIRO enforcement action for supervisory matters (violating securities/derivatives laws, regulations or CIRO requirements; aiding and abetting; failing to satisfy supervisory obligations).
- Behaviours/skills: assist Executives with periodic policies-and-procedures reviews; maintain adequate records of supervisory activity (business location reviews; compliance issues identified; resolution of identified issues); manage a supervision system balancing regulatory compliance and the dealer's business objectives; inform appropriate Executives of implementation issues; report allegations of serious misconduct to an appropriate Executive.

**Sub-competency III. Compliance function**
- Knowledge: role of the compliance department (setting policies and procedures; identifying, assessing, advising on, acting on, communicating, monitoring, escalating and reporting on regulatory compliance; CIRO expectations of dealer compliance functions and the role, responsibility and accountability of all dealer personnel; escalation responsibility).
- Behaviours/skills: keep up to date with relevant policy/procedure changes; implement compliance-function recommendations; ensure identified compliance issues are resolved and recorded; communicate and escalate non-compliance and red flags to compliance.

**Sub-competency IV. General responsibilities of Supervisors**
- Knowledge: general supervision requirements relevant to — a supervisory system providing reasonable assurance of compliance with CIRO requirements, securities and derivatives laws, regulations and other applicable laws; employees and Approved Persons; operation of account types; responsibilities and relative authority of Supervisors; shared office premises; sales practices; method, timing and distribution of compliance-related notices; controls for accessing and amending client records; supervisory action on a dealer's behalf; supervisory record-keeping requirements and retention periods (list of Supervisors, applicable qualifications, specific responsibilities and dates of designation; activity, review and resolution of identified compliance issues; supervisory reports; identification and resolution of compliance issues; terms of delegation and authority for Supervisors).
- Behaviours/skills: comply with relevant requirements, policies and procedures; ensure appropriate Executives approve applicable supervision policies and procedures; exercise authority to manage the dealer's activities, Approved Persons and employees; take effective and timely remedial action where matters fall or appear to fall outside the bounds of conduct, just and equitable principles of trade, good business practice or applicable rules and regulations; properly fulfil supervision assignments by fully applying and enforcing dealer policies and procedures; properly apply authority to resolve issues consistently with dealer policies and applicable regulatory requirements; provide written acknowledgment after reading and understanding relevant policies and procedures; participate in introductory and continuing education; support establishing, maintaining and enforcing timely updates to written policies and procedures regarding business and compliance conduct and supervision/compliance communications.

**Sub-competency V. Delegation and automation controls for supervisory tasks and activities**
- Knowledge: CIRO requirements for delegation of supervisory tasks, including specific functions that cannot be delegated or automated; documented delegation controls (detailed policies and procedures on how delegation works and to whom; escalation processes; factors triggering review by the delegator; examination of delegates' reviews; regular reports from delegate to delegator; regular — at minimum annual — reviews of the delegation process); automation as a form of delegation (how automation works and the Supervisor's role; what can/cannot be automated); controls, policies and procedures for automation (factors triggering manual review; periodic testing and auditing).
- Behaviours/skills: consider which supervisory tasks can be delegated while responsibility stays with the original Supervisor; maintain records of delegation terms and follow-up; ensure delegates are qualified, informed in writing, performing adequately and using issue-reporting mechanisms; periodically assess compensating controls; for automated processes — ensure the process identifies issues and routes activity for manual review where necessary; clearly articulate manual-review triggers; perform periodic testing/auditing; confirm automated approvals would have been approved manually; document testing and third-party oversight; ensure all required information is collected, complete and accurate; update automated processes and resolve issues needing compliance/technical input; set out delegator (Supervisor) roles and ensure delegator training on the function and use of the automated process (not the coding/technology); ensure the delegator approves the automation process and any changes and retains responsibility whether automation is used or not.

### Competency 3: Supervisory Structure: Specific Supervision Responsibilities
**Sub-competency I. Dealer business and operations** — Knowledge: policies and procedures specific to the dealer's supervision system; dealer's responsibility to appoint as many Supervisors as needed; the dealer's business model (types of products traded/offered; type of trading; locations of Supervisors and other Approved Persons; different types of Supervisors and their functions; relationship between supervisory system and internal controls); risks, opportunities and requirements of business models — advisory (roles of Approved Persons); portfolio management; managed account model (PM/APM roles); online (operations, regulatory obligations, product limitations; role of algorithms and modern portfolio theory; product limits such as ETFs; role of Approved Persons); OEO model (tools; DEA; CFDs); client types (retail; institutional including DEA); account types (advisory; in-house managed; third-party managed; discretionary; wrap; tax-deferred (registered and non-registered); margin; derivatives); basic security types (equities; mutual funds and ETFs; fixed income; specialized e.g. cryptocurrency); complex security types (leveraged and inverse ETFs; principal-protected notes; asset-backed securities); derivatives types (options; futures contract options; futures; forwards; swaps; CFDs; other similar derivatives as defined by CIRO); compensation structures (commission-based; fee-based; negotiated flat fee; fees for non-core activities; interest spread income; profitability measures); development, evaluation and delivery of products and services (product risk characteristics and appropriate accounts; industry initiatives, developments, new and existing rules; new-product due diligence procedures and ongoing risk assessment). Behaviours/skills: consider supervision-system policies; escalate concerns about critical numbers of Approved Persons/accounts under one Supervisor; fully and properly supervise each employee/Approved Person; keep current; consistently apply standards of conduct, just and equitable principles of trade and good business practices; consider scope/complexity of the business model (accounts and products; compensation; client types); keep supervisory notes (consolidating control, facilitating audit, flagging patterns); prioritize high-risk issues; monitor for red flags indicating possible insider trading and improper conduct; weigh risk significance against resource availability.

**Sub-competency II. Activities of Approved Persons engaged in dealing and advising** — Knowledge: supervisory requirements for new Registered Representatives and Investment Representatives; supervision of suitability determination obligations for retail and institutional clients; KYP obligations; borrowing-to-invest strategies; KYC requirements including client education and comprehension (client financial circumstances affecting income, net worth, assets and liabilities; investment knowledge; risk profile; objectives; time horizon; significance of KYC and its relationship to recommendations; relationship between objectives, risk tolerance, loss capacity, performance; full transparent disclosure of debts, liabilities, future goals and current cash flow/commitments; fluid nature of client circumstances); relevant competencies applicable to RRs/IRs, APMs or PMs. Behaviours/skills: ensure Approved Persons keep documentation/notes of material client discussions; ensure the assigned client-facing Approved Person exercises primary responsibility to update KYC and conduct suitability determinations; ensure compliance with KYC, KYP and suitability obligations; refer issues to another appropriate Supervisor as needed; escalate and follow up; consider high-risk client-facing Approved Persons; ensure the designated Supervisor completes, files and keeps the Monthly Supervision Report for regulatory review; consider specific challenges with vulnerable, retired or about-to-retire (senior) clients, including POAs, effective communication, diminished mental capacity and financial exploitation.

**Sub-competency III. Account approvals** — split by account class:
- (i) All accounts — Knowledge: account approval requirements with prescribed timelines for approvals, updates and changes (collection of client account records; business-day approval requirements; non-client account requirements; KYC and client identification requirements); client account record requirements (identity-verification documentation; account-appropriateness documentation; KYC per CIRO requirements; account application); account appropriateness requirements (putting prospective clients' interests first; appropriate scope/selection of products, services and account relationships; purpose and content of Relationship Disclosure — products/services/account types including fee-based, leverage and margin; limitations; fees and compensation guidelines; account maintenance conditions; wealth management process; performance benchmarks; impact of fees, turnover and taxes on managed product returns; client relationship model; systematic approaches to investment management; leverage risk disclosure statement); partnership, trust and corporation account requirements; requirements for cash accounts, margin accounts and extending margin; margin account agreement (full rights/obligations list as in syllabus 4.7). Behaviours/skills: record reviews/approvals and how information was obtained and verified; ensure records collected before approval; verify identity/due diligence, insider status, creditworthiness when financing; comply with prescribed approval timelines and apply restrictions on non-compliant new accounts; obtain written approval from the other dealer and designate non-client status before opening accounts for employees of other CIRO dealers; verify KYC accuracy and internal consistency; verify notes/records/documents on account opening (fulsome records; guarantor name/address; signed trading authorization; record of persons with trading authorization in sufficient detail to identify persons with authorization over multiple clients/accounts); verify timely client information updates; on Approved Person change, ensure verification of account application information and written acknowledgment of review/update.
- (ii) Retail client accounts — Knowledge: supervision procedures. Behaviours/skills: verify the appropriateness of the account.
- (iii) Institutional client accounts — Knowledge: definition of institutional client; suitability obligation; exemption-qualifying clients; direct market access account and supervision requirements; circumstances where a qualifying hedger can be classified as institutional. Behaviours/skills: ensure the client qualifies as an institutional client.
- (iv) OEO accounts — Knowledge: account appropriateness (incapacity to conduct online activities; investor seeking advice); product due diligence; addressing conflicts in clients' best interests; restrictions on automated derivatives account approvals (CFDs; OTC derivatives; futures or similar strategies; options strategies other than low-risk long calls, long puts, covered calls or protective puts); ongoing supervision of automated approvals; rules-based approval process and manual-review triggers (non-individual applicant; under age of majority; trading authorization used; derivatives strategies without appropriate knowledge/experience; insider). Behaviours/skills: ensure written disclosure that the dealer will not provide recommendations and the client is solely responsible for all investment decisions, and will not make suitability or appropriateness determinations (considering current financial situation, investment knowledge, objectives and time horizon, risk profile, portfolio composition, risk levels and similar factors); raise issues on the adequacy of compensating controls for automated account opening.
- (v) Derivatives trading accounts — Knowledge: full derivatives trading agreement content (dealer rights/obligations; client obligations/acknowledgments; options/futures specifics; cumulative loss limits; Letter of Undertaking for institutional clients; hedger classification). Behaviours/skills: verify provision/receipt of CIRO-approved derivatives disclosure statement, positive acknowledgment, amendments and a record of names/addresses/dates; verify KYC consistency (e.g., accredited investor status); determine appropriateness of intended strategies against personal/financial circumstances, needs/objectives, knowledge, risk profile, time horizon and the client's interests; restrict inappropriate strategies, record and share restrictions; determine hedger qualification (business risks; same/materially related transactions; intended effect; hedge effectiveness evaluation methods).
- (vi) Managed accounts — Knowledge: supervision requirements (designated Supervisor; specific policies and procedures; managed account agreement before opening; written approval per account; record retention; fair-allocation policy to client); managed account agreement purpose/content; specific conflicts rules; fees and remuneration rules. Behaviours/skills: verify KYC consistency; ensure individuals authorized for managed accounts are a PM, APM, or a registered/approved/exempted sub-advisor under a written sub-advisor agreement (registered/approved or exempt in their jurisdiction; subject to at-least-equivalent conflict-of-interest provisions or contractual compliance).
- (vii) Discretionary accounts — Knowledge: supervision requirements (designated qualified Supervisor(s); specific policies and procedures; identification in books and records; agreement before acceptance; designated approval of account and agreement; record retention); agreement purpose/content/disclosures (extent and restrictions of discretionary authority; 12-month non-renewable maximum; termination rules; conflicts rules including prohibition on holding publicly traded securities of the dealer or affiliates; responsible-person prohibitions). Behaviours/skills: ensure an RR may only be authorized to effect discretionary trades if they have at least two years of active experience trading/advising/analyzing all product types to be traded on a discretionary basis, the account is maintained at the dealer for which the RR conducts business, and the account is used only on a temporary basis.

**Sub-competency IV. Account activity** — split by account class:
- (i) All accounts — Knowledge: supervision structures and models, including the two-tier review model; account activity supervision policies and procedures (identifying high-risk clients to the dealer; identifying clients at high risk of improper market activities; AML/ATF compliance; controls over accessing and amending client records); supervisory measures for Approved Persons with regulatory-infraction histories; risks of direct order entry without intermediation (OEO); requirements and conditions for temporary holds to prevent financial exploitation of vulnerable clients. Behaviours/skills: take effective and timely remedial action; maintain audit trails and supervisory notes; ensure recommendations are presently and continuously suitable; ensure client-facing compliance with suitability obligations; select accounts for review using criteria providing reasonable assurance of detecting improper trading; apply policies for reviewing activity and dealing with identified issues; confirm dealer expectations of supervisory staff; identify and escalate supervisory-structure gaps (including adequacy of first-tier supervision where two-tier applies); identify and resolve other client matters (complaints; cash account violations; transfers between unrelated accounts or between non-client and client accounts or deposits from non-client to client accounts; trading while under-margined); ensure no discretionary trading (including time and price discretion) by those trading on a dealer's behalf except in proper discretionary accounts.
- (ii) Retail client accounts — Knowledge: policies addressing daily and monthly supervision of retail trading activity; actions on identified issues; costs/ticket charges in fee-based accounts; transactional information requirements; daily supervision including red-flag review; suitability determination triggers; monthly supervision including specified account types; detection of concerning/suspicious practices (full list: unsuitable trading; undue concentration; excessive trading; restricted securities trading; client-facing AP conflicts; excessive transfers/cancellations; inappropriate/high-risk strategies; deterioration of holdings quality; excessive or improper crosses; improper/excessive employee trading; front running); cross-supervision; client mail account procedures. Behaviours/skills: pre-order and pre-recommendation suitability due diligence; portfolio suitability on non-trade triggering events (deposit/withdrawal/transfer of securities, derivatives or bullion; change of responsible Approved Person; material changes to client circumstances/objectives revising KYC); readily accessible account activity information (receipts; deposits; withdrawals; journal entries); apply policies regarding account number changes, late payment, outstanding margin calls, undisclosed short sales, manipulative and deceptive activities, insider trading; review pre- or post-trade; cover all retail account types; detect activity patterns not apparent in single trades; non-trade issues (late payments; margin problems; trade cancellations; suspicious transfers/flows); risk-based selection for post-trade review (clients with access to material non-public information; control-block holders; market professionals); timely review; Supervisor access to higher-risk-client information; technology for selecting activity; consider compensation practices and associated conflicts; detect non-compliant, concerning or suspicious practices.
- (iii) Institutional client accounts — Knowledge: requirements and exemptions for institutional suitability determinations; policies for supervision/review of institutional trading and remediation; detection of improper/suspicious activity (manipulative and deceptive activities; restricted-list trading in securities and derivatives; front-running by employee or proprietary accounts; restricted-transfer securities and derivatives; exceeding derivatives position or exercise limits); DMA account and supervision requirements. Behaviours/skills: evaluate whether the client can independently evaluate investment risk and exercises independent judgment (written/oral understanding on reliance; pattern of acceptance of recommendations; use of ideas from other dealers/professionals/issuers; use of multiple dealers/PMs/advisors; general market experience; instrument-specific experience including ability to evaluate market developments and ancillary risks such as currency risk; complexity of the instruments); obtain appropriate waivers based on sophistication determination; ensure compliance with institutional suitability requirements; detect non-compliant, concerning or suspicious practices.
- (iv) OEO accounts — Knowledge: OEO exemptions and requirements, including review policies and direct-entry risks. Behaviours/skills: ensure no recommendations result from the client having an account with a separate legal entity, separate business unit, or the dealer itself; ensure proper general supervision reviews separate from suitability; monitor compliance with all OEO requirements; ensure Approved Persons provide no recommendation to purchase, sell, hold or exchange any security (including any class of security or security of a class of issuer), derivatives or precious metals bullion.
- (v) Derivatives trading accounts — Knowledge: CIRO requirements for handling derivatives accounts; CFD undertakings; designated and alternate Supervisor responsibilities where continuous supervision is required; retail detection requirements (excessive intra-day/short-term transactions; under-margined transacting; beyond margin/credit limits; exceeding approved loss limits; exceeding position/exercise limits; speculative transactions in hedging accounts; restricted-list derivatives; restricted-transfer derivatives; derivatives to avoid insider trading restrictions; uncovered option exposures; delivery-month exposures); client notification policies (approaching expiry; significant derivative changes from underlying changes; dealer business policy changes; new trading/regulatory developments); policies requiring Supervisor approval for solicitation; preventing transactions without executed agreements; handling pending delivery months; insider-related derivatives trading; the 0.5%-of-float CFD restriction; prohibiting CFDs conferring delivery or shareholder rights; client access to qualified Approved Persons during normal business hours. Behaviours/skills: ensure derivatives handling compliance; ensure continuing suitability and client-interest-first; review and approve annual client loss limits considering previous losses; review all discretionary and managed derivatives accounts daily and monthly; ensure proficiency of those trading/advising; ensure pre-initial-trade client requirements (approval of solicitation; completed derivatives account application; signed derivatives trading agreement; most recent risk disclosure; written record of account approver); determine strategy risk appropriateness; restrict, note and communicate restrictions; ensure compliance with trading/clearing entity requirements and position/exercise limits.
- (vi) Managed accounts — Knowledge: policies for supervising individuals handling managed accounts; conflict-of-interest requirements; fair allocation; direct supervision of Approved Persons with conditions/exceptions; managed account committee requirements and responsibilities. Behaviours/skills: review each managed account for reasonable assurance the client's investment objectives are pursued and handling complies with CIRO requirements; escalate detected conflict-of-interest non-compliance; ensure APM supervision (including pre-approval of advice) by a PM at the dealer or another dealer authorized to provide discretionary management and not in a period of close supervision, or an Approved Person contracted to provide supervision; ensure the management committee at least annually reviews managed-account supervision policies and recommends actions to senior management; monitor Approved Persons under close or strict supervision; review derivatives managed accounts daily and monthly; ensure proficiency; ensure no improper discretionary trading.
- (vii) Discretionary accounts — Knowledge: the designated Supervisor must not delegate the performance of reviews. Behaviours/skills: review financial performance of each discretionary account at least monthly; assess whether the RR should continue effecting discretionary trades based on performance; review derivatives discretionary accounts daily and monthly; review any discretionary order initiated by an RR prior to entry unless the RR is approved as a PM, the RR is also an Executive, or a designated Supervisor reviews the order within the prescribed timeframe; review discretionary orders initiated by an Executive approved as a PM no later than the day after the trade; ensure no improper discretionary trading; ensure proficiency.

**Sub-competency V. Trading and market rules** — Knowledge: market rules including UMIR where applicable (best execution; manipulative and deceptive practices; disruptive trading practices; fair and equitable trading practices; securities industry code of ethics, standards and dealer guidelines; order placement/settlement/delivery; order error/change handling; cash account rule purpose and application; settlement procedures; overdue cash account restrictions; order confirmation requirements including fees/commissions; trade confirmation requirements/guidelines/best practices; types of buy/sell/short-sell orders; long and short margin accounts and special margin situations; need for specialized trading authorizations); applicable regulatory standards for review, acceptance and approval of orders; trading supervisory systems (identification of requirements; documentation; training and proficiency; delegation of supervisory and compliance personnel; violation procedures; reviewing systems; documenting and retaining compliance review results; Board reports); regular compliance review of marketplace trading provisions (audit trail; electronic access; specific unacceptable activities; manipulative and deceptive activities; restricted securities; grey-list securities; disclosure; frontrunning; client/principal trading; client priority; best execution; order exposure; time synchronization); risk-based supervision system factors (size — revenue, market share, market exposure, trade volume; organizational structure; number/location of offices; product/service complexity; registrants per location; disciplinary history; risk profile and red flags); gatekeeping responsibilities (typical client financial activity/patterns to identify suspicious transactions; whistleblower frameworks; reporting obligations to dealers and regulators). Behaviours/skills: adopt, document and maintain a risk management/supervisory controls system reasonably designed to manage financial, regulatory and other risks of marketplace access and automated order system use (by Participant, client or Access Person); document each step of the compliance review process (reviewer; dates; information sources including triggering alert; samples and selection criteria; queries to trader/client/order handlers; results; escalation measures; corrective actions).

**Sub-competency VI. Advertisements, sales literature and correspondence** — Knowledge: review/supervision policies and procedures; document retention and supervisory record-keeping; pre-use/publication approval requirements (research reports; market letters; telemarketing scripts; promotional seminar texts — excluding educational seminar texts; original advertisements or original template advertisements; material containing performance reports or summaries used to solicit clients); when post-use review and sampling are permitted. Behaviours/skills: consider social media/interactive platforms used by employees/Approved Persons, verify unauthorized platform use and escalate; consider what constitutes the dealer's advertisement, sales literature and correspondence and the requirements for various public communication methods; ensure the dealer does not issue, participate in or knowingly allow its name in content that — contains an untrue statement or omission of material fact or is otherwise false or misleading; contains an unjustified promise of specific results; uses unrepresentative statistics to suggest unwarranted or exaggerated conclusions or fails to identify material assumptions; contains any opinion or forecast of future events not clearly labeled as such; fails to fairly present potential risks to the client; is detrimental to the interests of the public, CIRO or its regulated dealers; fails to comply with CIRO requirements or applicable laws.

**Sub-competency VII. Research reports** — Knowledge: policies and procedures specifically addressing — designation of one or more Supervisors to review and approve research reports; prohibition on research reports or opinions for own securities or holding company; cross-supervision; minimum disclosures; conduct of analysts; publishing research reports; recommendations by analysts; conflict-of-interest disclosure requirements. Behaviours/skills: ensure disclosure of any matter reasonably indicating an existing or potential conflict for the dealer or analyst; ensure each report complies with disclosure requirements, including — dealer/affiliate beneficial ownership of 1%+ of any class of the subject issuer's equity securities (as of the end of the month prior to issuance, or the second most recent month if issuance is within 10 days of month-end); issuer securities held directly or indirectly by the analyst, an associate of the analyst, or any person directly involved in preparing the report; services provided by any partner, Director or officer of the dealer or the analyst within one year before the report/recommendation; investment banking services for remuneration within one year; the name of any partner, Director, officer, employee or agent of the dealer who is a partner, Director, officer or employee of the issuer or serves in an equivalent advisory capacity; market-making in any equity or equity-related security of the subject issuer.

**Sub-competency VIII. Risks associated with dealer activity and registered locations** — Knowledge: business location supervision guidelines (scope; planning; business location audit program and training; risk identification; audit report and follow-up); internal risk factors (quality of management and staff; knowledge and experience; adequacy of resources; key person reliance; fraudulent or unethical behaviour); reputational risk (performance incentive/compensation structures — suitability-vs-reward conflicts; churning; overcharging via excessive fixed-income mark-ups with limited commission transparency; litigation and client complaints; community-specific marketing strategy and sales practices); business activity risk (exposure inherent to a product/service at a location; lines of business and aggregate portfolio risk; risks of individual RR conduct); business location size risk (risk-weighting; risk assessment and control practices; growth rate; number of registrants; type of registrants e.g. enhanced supervision); client base risk (obligations to clients — protecting clients from inappropriate actions by the location and its representatives; the location's risk that clients use it for inappropriate actions); retail distribution risk (sale of complex/opaque products including illiquid or alternative products; advisory services and fully discretionary managed products — loss of control over books and records, particularly client address changes; failure to meet regulatory standards for supervision and control, including inadequate control/review of advertising and sales literature and lack of documented evidence of overall supervision; failure to meet client margin management requirements; inadequate follow-up on compliance audit findings; non-competitive products or services; new or complex products not understood by retail investors; AP failure to meet regulatory, contractual and legal obligations and high levels of integrity; professional skills and knowledge in client dealings; improper reassignment of terminated registrants' accounts; commission-based compensation conflicts; inadequate control over issuance of customized portfolio summaries; losing APs to local competitors creating economic, litigation and reputational risks because clients frequently follow their Approved Persons); local business strategy and decision risk. Behaviours/skills: consider the risk profile(s) of relevant business locations (Supervisor experience level; tenure/turnover of personnel; internal/external disciplinary history or regulatory actions; revenue sources; number of client accounts; percentage of revenue from higher-risk investments; prior compliance audit findings); ensure management and control of specific risks are assigned to qualified individuals with sufficient resources; identify red flags, follow up and escalate.

## PART 5 — RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN SYLLABUS AND COMPETENCY PROFILE (as stated by CIRO)

- Bulletin 25-0212 (verbatim): **"Each syllabus reflects the related competency profile, which was developed through a rigorous job task analysis, to ensure the validity of the examinations."** Also: "Each syllabus includes the relevant learning outcomes with appropriate taxonomy, parameters, and weighting for each exam"; the syllabus is developed with a Syllabus Committee of internal and external SMEs and approved by CIRO; it "informs the coverage and level of difficulty for all examination questions," validated by an Exam Committee; exams undergo piloting, psychometric analysis, and psychometrically recognized standard setting; the practice exam is "an equivalent form of the exam" drawn from an extensive question bank, with 5 additional forms per exam type. Exams developed with support from **Fitch Learning**.

## PART 6 — CONTEXT: the new proficiency model
- New model effective **January 1, 2026** (Bulletin 25-0110, April 17, 2025; CSA-approved Amendments to IDPC Rules 2500–2800). Shift "from a course centric model with exams tied to courses, to an assessment centric model without pre-requisite courses, with some mandatory education and experience."
- The Supervisor Exam is **one of nine Proficiency exams** (CIRE; Retail Securities; Institutional Securities; Director and Executive; Supervisor; Chief Compliance Officer; Chief Financial Officer; Derivatives; Trader) on the Exam Hub: https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/exam-hub.
- Supervisors additionally require minimum **two-year relevant experience**, with relevance depending on the supervisory role, activities and the dealer's business model (Guidance on Relevant Education and Experience Requirements; IDPC Rule 2600).
- Competency profile development history (Bulletin 23-0138): Phase I — RRs and IRs (consulted Aug 18, 2020); Phase II — Executives, Directors, UDPs, CCOs, CFOs (Aug 31, 2021); Phase III — **Supervisors**, Traders, APMs and PMs (consulted Aug 29, 2022); updated and finalized Sept 25, 2023; refreshed copies (Derivatives Modernization updates) published with Bulletin 25-0212 on July 21, 2025 and hosted on the Competency Hub.
