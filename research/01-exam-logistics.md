# CIRO Supervisor Exam — Official Exam Logistics (New Proficiency Model, effective January 1, 2026)

Research date: June 4, 2026. All facts below come from official sources: ciro.ca, the Fitch Learning Support site (fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com — CIRO's contracted exam administrator; policies carry "Copyright © 2026 CIRO"), prometric.com (CIRO's contracted delivery vendor), and the amended IDPC Rules. Anything not confirmable from an official source is flagged at the end.

## 1. Exam format

From the official **Supervisor Exam Syllabus** (PDF, "Last updated May 29, 2025"), exam parameters table, quoted verbatim — https://www.ciro.ca/media/13606/download?attachment (linked from https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/exam-hub/supervisor-exam):

- "Exams: **1 paper**"
- "Exam format: **Proctored (remote or in person)**"
- "Exam duration: **3 hours**"
- "Question format: **Multiple choice: • 60 Standard • 30 Item set**"
- "Questions per exam: **90**"
- "Attempts allowed per exam: **3**"
- Question weighting ("Indicative Qs" per element): 1 General regulatory framework — 10; 2 Supervisory structure: Investment Dealer responsibilities — 10; 3 Business and operations — 9; 4 Account approvals — 20; 5 Account activity — 15; 6 Activities of Approved Persons — 8; 7 Trading and market rules — 6; 8 Advertisements, sales literature and communications and research reports — 7; 9 Risks associated with Investment Dealer activity and registered locations — 5. (Sums to 90.)
- Taxonomy: questions are written at Remember / Understand / Apply / Analyze levels; "syllabus terms are cumulative."
- Computer-based: delivered on computer at Prometric test centres ("a Prometric staff member will lead you to your assigned computer in the secure exam area" — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5188792447263-When-should-I-arrive-at-the-test-centre) or via Prometric "ProProctor Live" remote proctoring (Remote Exam Policy PDF — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5227146510367-Remote-Exam-Policy).
- Proctoring: "During the exam, Candidates will be observed and monitored in person and through continuous live video by the Proctor" (test centre); remote = "continuous live video, screen sharing and audio feeds" with a Readiness Agent + Proctor (Remote Exam Policy). Test centres are in Canada only; "To take CIRO Exams outside of Canada requires the candidate to take the exam remotely" (Test Centre Exam Guide — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5186242715167-Test-Centre-Exam-Guide). Remote exams "can be taken globally."
- Languages: "CIRO is planning to launch 9 different exams, **each in English and French**" (Bulletin 25-0205 — https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/ciro-proficiency-model-approved-persons-under-investment-dealer-and-partially-consolidated-rules-1). "Yes, all of the CIRO Exams are available in French and can be taken in French at any test centre location. French speaking personnel will only be available at test centers in Québec" (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5188825234591). French also available by remote exam with French-speaking Prometric support (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5199211201439). Language must be selected at enrolment/booking; "You will not be able to make the change on the day of the exam or with Prometric" (Test Centre Exam Guide). Test Centre Policy: "Test Centre services are available in English at all locations and French at select locations."

## 2. Passing score / pass mark

- **CIRO does not publish a pass mark.** Official FAQ ("Will CIRO publish the minimum passing grade?"): "No. Passing grades exist to ensure the minimally competent candidate can achieve a pass on the exam... there are different versions of each exam, each with its own passing grade. As such, publishing one passing grade will be misleading. Upon completion of an exam, individuals will receive a pass or fail mark." — https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/new-candidates
- "Candidates receive a Pass or Fail result immediately after completing their test. Fitch Learning only administers the CIRO proficiency exams and does not have access to detailed scoring information." — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5365740605471-What-is-the-required-grade-to-pass-an-exam
- Method: "Standard setting will be done according to psychometrically recognized methods. Standard setting is the methodology used to define a pass-score for each examination." — Bulletin 25-0212 — https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/ciro-proficiency-model-approved-persons-under-investment-dealer-and-partially-consolidated-rules-2
- Results: "Exam results will be provided immediately upon completion of the exam to the Candidate and will be available for review on the Candidate portal within 24 hours. Candidates will be provided with their overall result (pass/fail/absent). An absent result is considered a failed result." — Exam Enrolment and Attempts Policy PDF, https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5227155152287

- "**CIRO has set its examination fee at $475 per exam** based on a cost recovery model. This fee will be the same for all exam types." — Bulletin 25-0205 (July 14, 2025), https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/ciro-proficiency-model-approved-persons-under-investment-dealer-and-partially-consolidated-rules-1
- Fees, Payments and Refunds Policy (v2, issued April 2026): "The **Enrolment Fee for each exam is currently set as 475 CAD**. The **Retake Fee for each exam is currently set as 300 CAD**." "Unless otherwise specified, all fees presented in this policy are subject to applicable taxes." — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5227129709087
- "The fee for the first exam attempt in an enrolment period is $475... subject to a retake fee of $300 for each second and third attempt." — https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/new-candidates
- Payment: "Payments are to be made in full using **secured credit card payment only** via the provided Enrolment site. Neither CIRO nor Fitch Learning can take payments via any other method or channel." (Fees Policy). Refunds only for defined Exceptional Circumstances or payment-in-error reported within 24 hours; approved refunds processed within 28 days. Mandatory Conduct Training is free ("will not be subject to any fees" — Bulletin 25-0205).

## 5. Eligibility / prerequisites

- **No prerequisite courses** for any exam: "CIRO is shifting from a course centric model with exams tied to courses, to an assessment centric model without pre-requisite courses" (Bulletin 25-0212). "The new proficiency model does not require preparatory courses" (Exam Hub).
- **Enrolment eligibility** (Exam Enrolment and Attempts Policy, verbatim attestation): candidates must be the age of majority in their jurisdiction and attest: "I am enrolling for the exam either (A) to demonstrate competency with respect to the work that I do or intend to do at a CIRO Dealer Member, and... either (i) work for a CIRO Dealer Member in a role where these competencies are required, or (ii) plan to apply within the next 12 months to work for a CIRO Dealer Member in a role where these competencies are required, or (B) because I intend to pursue further CIRO exams for which this exam is a prerequisite." "Individuals who do not meet the conditions above, such as education providers, are not eligible to enrol." So **dealer sponsorship is NOT required to write the exam** — intent to apply within 12 months suffices — but sponsorship by a CIRO Investment Dealer IS required to be approved: "In order to apply to be a CIRO Approved Person, an individual must be sponsored by a CIRO Investment Dealer" (https://www.ciro.ca/registered-individuals/proficiency/new-candidates).
- **Is the CIRE a prerequisite for the Supervisor Exam?** Not for enrolling/writing it. For approval as a Supervisor, the amended IDPC Rule 2603(1)(i)(i) (Appendix 1, https://www.ciro.ca/media/12626/download?attachment) states verbatim: "**Supervisor: Supervisor Exam, and (I) If supervising trading in, or accounts for those who trade in securities, derivatives, or managed accounts, the exam requirements applicable to the individuals being supervised listed in sub-clauses 2603(1)(i)(a) to (f), and (h) except: (A) the Canadian Investment Regulatory Exam is not required if the Supervisor satisfies the experience requirements in sub-clause 2603(1)(ii)(d)**." So a Supervisor must also pass the exams of those they supervise (e.g., CIRE + Retail Securities Exam for retail RR supervision; + Derivatives Exam if supervising derivatives) — but the CIRE component is waived if they meet the experience requirement.
- **Experience requirement** — 2603(1)(ii)(d): "Supervisor: **Minimum two years of relevant experience acceptable to the Corporation**."
- Exemptions: Rule 2625(1) — supervisors of mutual-funds-only representatives can substitute (e.g., former MFDA branch manager within 90 days, or IFSE Mutual Fund Branch Managers' Exam / CSI Branch Compliance Officers Course instead of the Supervisor Exam). Existing Approved Persons approved before January 1, 2026 continuing in the same role are grandfathered (Rule 2625(2); 180-day continuity rule) except mandatory Conduct Training — GN-2600-25-001, https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/guidance-new-ciro-proficiency-model-approved-persons-investment-dealers-exemptions-recognition (PDF mirror: https://www.bcsc.bc.ca/-/media/PWS/New-Resources/Industry/Marketplaces-SROs-and-Market-Infrastructure/SROs/2025/CIRO-Guidance-Note-Proficiency-Model-for-Approved-Persons-of-Investment-Dealers-April-172025.pdf). FINRA-related exemptions exist for CIRE/Derivatives Exam (Rule 2627), including for "a Supervisor over these individuals" re: the Derivatives Exam (Series 3 + Series 7).

## 6. Attempt limits and waiting periods

From the Exam Enrolment and Attempts Policy (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5227155152287) and CIRO FAQ pages:

- "**Enrolment Period**: the period from the date of Exam Enrolment up to a **maximum of 12 months**, during which the Candidate may complete **up to three Exam Attempts** for a specific exam."
- Retakes: "$300 for each second and third attempt" (CIRO New Candidates FAQ); resit purchased via a link in the Candidate Portal Exams tab after a fail (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5188767120543).
- "**Cooling-off Period**: the **six-month** period that occurs after **three failed (or absent) Exam Attempts within six months**. During this time, Exam Enrolment, scheduling and further Exam Attempts are not permitted." CIRO FAQ: "There will be a cooling off period of 6 months, associated with those that attempt and fail an exam three times in a 6-month period."
- After 3 attempts or expiry of 12 months: pay a new $475 Enrolment Fee to start a new Enrolment Period, "subject to the Cooling-off Period requirement."
- Rescheduling: free if done "**at least 36 hours** prior to the time of the scheduled exam" — does not count as an attempt. No-show without 36-hour notice = "**absent (fail)** result... and no refund." Candidates can switch test centre ↔ remote up to 36 hours before, free, self-managed in the Candidate Portal (Test Centre Exam Guide). Exceptional Circumstances exemptions (illness with medical certificate, family illness, death of immediate family, natural disaster, delivery failure) can add an attempt or extend the period; "Work demands, general weather conditions... are not considered Exceptional Circumstances." Requests within 7 working days with evidence.
- "Enrolments are not transferable between individuals or different CIRO Exam types."

## 7. Validity period and post-approval requirements

- **Exam validity — 3 years** (IDPC Rule 2628, quoted in GN-2600-25-001): an individual is deemed to have successfully completed an exam if (i) they "successfully completed the prescribed exam **within three years prior to the date of application for approval**," OR (ii) they were "previously approved in the same Approved Person category, or another category which required the same exam, within three years prior to the application for approval," OR (iii) they "gained **one year of relevant securities industry experience**, acceptable to the Corporation, within three years prior to the application for approval." Suspended periods don't count as approved (2628(2)). Validity periods do not apply to the CFA Charter / CIM designation (2628(3)).
- **Post-approval**: every Approved Person (including Supervisors) must complete CIRO-prescribed **Conduct Training within 30 days of approval** (Rule 2604(2)(i)); existing Approved Persons by December 31, 2026 (2604(2)(ii)); free of charge; failure → automatic suspension (Rule 2552(2)), reinstated on completion + notification. Dealer Member training obligations also apply (Rule 2604(1)). Continuing education: under amended Rule 2704, "Supervisor — Compliance course requirement: Yes; Professional development course requirement: No" (each CE cycle). Sources: GN-2600-25-001 and Appendix 1 clean rules (https://www.ciro.ca/media/12626/download?attachment).

## 8. Approval categories covered — one exam for all supervisor types?

- **Yes — there is a single Supervisor Exam for the single "Supervisor" approval category.** Bulletin 25-0110: amendments "**Streamlined continuing education requirements for all Supervisors, to reflect development of a single competency profile for all Supervisors**" — https://www.ciro.ca/newsroom/publications/ciro-proficiency-model-approved-persons-under-investment-dealer-and-partially-consolidated-rules-0. The Supervisor competency profile is one document (Appendix 8 — https://www.ciro.ca/media/1006/download?attachment).
- But the Supervisor Exam alone is not always sufficient for approval: per Rule 2603(1)(i)(i), the Supervisor must ALSO have passed the exams applicable to those being supervised (Retail Securities, Institutional Securities, Derivatives, and/or PM designations per 2603(1)(i)(a)–(f),(h)), with the CIRE waived if the 2-year experience requirement (2603(1)(ii)(d)) is met. The Supervisor Exam does not qualify you for any other approval category (RR, Trader, CCO, CFO, etc. each have their own exams).

## 9. Exam rules — what you can bring, scratch paper, calculator, reference materials

- **Test centre** ("What can I take with me on exam day?" — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5188773453215): must disclose all items at check-in; small lockers provided (no access during exam or breaks). Allowed without notice: Prometric's pre-approved items (medical/religious items, head coverings, eyeglasses without case, foam earplugs without string, etc. — https://www.prometric.com/pre-approved-items) and "Water - in a clear and transparent container with a lid or cap. All labels must be removed." Prometric may provide: noise-reducing headset or earplugs, tissue. **Not permitted in the secure exam area**: "Outerwear, Hats, Keys, Wallet, Purses/Briefcase, Large bags/Backpacks, **Documentation of any kind**, Jewelry, including watches (wedding rings are permitted), Phones/smartphones/wearable technology." So **no reference materials of any kind** — closed-book.
- **Scratch paper**: For remote exams — "No. You are not permitted to have paper and a pen during the exam. To make notes, you must use the **scratch pad function within the ProProctor platform**." — https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5199180904863. (Test centre: "Documentation of any kind" is prohibited; physical note materials are not on the permitted list.)
- **Breaks**: unscheduled breaks permitted but "the timer of your exam will still count down. It does not pause" (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5188809603615); remote: "Unscheduled breaks of no longer than 15 minutes can be taken... the timing of the exam will not be paused" (Remote Exam Policy).
- **ID**: valid, original, unexpired government-issued ID with clear photo AND signature, matching the candidate-portal name (both Test Centre and Remote Exam Policies). Arrive **30 minutes early** at test centres; "Late Candidates may not be permitted to take the exam."
- **Conduct**: Exam Candidate Conduct Policy (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5227155761439) prohibits cheating, unauthorized devices/aids, content disclosure, impersonation, etc.; penalties include halting the exam, revoking results, conditions, temporary/permanent enrolment bans, and possible CIRO regulatory action.
- Accommodations: a formal Accommodations Policy exists (https://fitchlearning-na.zendesk.com/hc/en-ca/articles/5227138655903); medications, medical devices, food/drink other than water, and non-pre-approved items require approved accommodations.
